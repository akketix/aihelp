This wiki is a community-driven common good. Because information in the field of Artificial Intelligence evolves rapidly, we operate under a **Shared Accountability Model** to maintain content safety and quality.

---

## Our Commitment: "Reasonable" Due Diligence

As maintainers, we employ reasonable due diligence to ensure the content served here is accurate, helpful, and safe:

1. **Automated Verification**: Every submission is tested against syntax linters to prevent formatting errors and broken links.
2. **The AI Council**: We run automated AI agents that cross-examine submissions against live search results to flag factual errors, hallucinations, and outdated API formats.
3. **Human Triaging**: Maintainers review the AI reports and manually inspect pull requests before merging changes into the production site.

> [!WARNING]
> While we perform reasonable due diligence, **our checks are not infallible.** AI models can make errors, and technical specs can change overnight. The wiki content is provided "as is" without warranties of any kind.

---

## User Responsibility: Shared Accountability

To keep this platform viable, users and readers must uphold their end of the shared accountability model:

### 1. Execute Your Own Due Diligence

- **Verify Before Deploying**: Never copy-paste code snippets, commands, or configuration files from this wiki directly into production systems without verifying them first against official documentation or isolated test environments.
- **Critical Consumption**: Treat all guides as educational references rather than absolute, production-grade instructions.

### 2. Responsible Contribution

If you are submitting edits or articles:

- **Fact-Check Your Claims**: Do not submit guess-work or unverified assertions. Provide source links to official documentation where possible.
- **No Malicious Payloads**: Do not submit code or procedures that compromise security or promote spam.

### 3. Active Correction

- If you notice a typo, an outdated version, or a technical error, you are accountable for helping correct it. Use the on-site **"Edit Page on Website"** button to submit a quick revision.
